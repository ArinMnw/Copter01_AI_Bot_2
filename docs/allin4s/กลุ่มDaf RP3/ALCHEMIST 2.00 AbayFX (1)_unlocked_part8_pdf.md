## 📄 หน้า 1: DAILY BIAS WEEKLY PROFILES

### 1. ส่วนของข้อความ
DAILY BIAS
WEEKLY PROFILES

1. INTRODUCTION TO WEEKLY PROFILES
WHEN USING WEEKLY PROFILES, WE ARE ESSENTIALLY ANTICIPATING HOW THE WEEKLY CANDLE IS LIKELY TO FORM BY USING EXPECTATIONS DERIVED FROM THE WEEKLY CHART AND APPLYING THEM TO THE DAILY TIMEFRAME.
IN SIMPLE TERMS, WE ARE LOOKING FOR THE HIGH OR LOW OF THE WEEK TO BE FORMED FIRST, BEFORE PRICE REVERSES IN THE OPPOSITE DIRECTION TO COMPLETE THE WEEKLY CANDLE.
THIS PROCESS PROVIDES US WITH A DIRECTIONAL BIAS UNTIL PRICE REACHES AN OPPOSING HIGHER TIME FRAME (HTF) LEVEL.
BY UNDERSTANDING HOW THE WEEKLY RANGE TYPICALLY DEVELOPS, WE CAN:
SET CLEAR DAILY EXPECTATIONS
MAINTAIN NARRATIVE ALIGNMENT
AVOID TRADING AGAINST THE WEEKLY STRUCTURE

2. SMT DIVERGENCE
SMT DIVERGENCE IS A HIGHLY EFFECTIVE TOOL WHEN USED ALONGSIDE WEEKLY PROFILES.
ITS ROLE IS TO:
CONFIRM THE HIGH OF THE WEEK IN A BEARISH CONTEXT
CONFIRM THE LOW OF THE WEEK IN A BULLISH CONTEXT
SMT ACTS AS STRONG CONFLUENCE BY HIGHLIGHTING RELATIVE WEAKNESS OR STRENGTH BETWEEN CORRELATED MARKETS.

(คำอธิบาย Classic Expansion, Midweek Reversal, Consolidation -> Thursday Reversal, TGI Friday เป็นภาษาอังกฤษ ซึ่งมีเนื้อหาแปลเหมือนหน้า 13 ของ part 7)

WEEKLY PROFILES EXAMPLE
Catatan Singkat
Garis vertikal menandakan hari Senin, atau hari Selasa jika hari Senin libur.
Untuk TGI Friday, perhatikan minggu dengan Classic Expansion yang biasanya diakhiri dengan pullback pada hari Jumat.
Weekly Profiles jika digabungkan dengan konsep sebelumnya seperti ERL/IRL, Candle Body Close, SMT Divergence, dan Institutional Order Flow (OF) dapat mengurangi subjektivitas dan memberikan kerangka kerja yang jelas dalam trading.

(คำอธิบายกราฟในส่วนล่างเป็นภาษาอินโดนีเซีย: Classic Expansion - Low of the Week, Perilaku Tengah Minggu, Thursday Reversal, Classic Expansion - High of the Week, Perilaku Hari Jumat)

QUICK NOTES
THE VERTICAL LINES REPRESENT MONDAY, OR TUESDAY IN CASES WHERE MONDAY IS A HOLIDAY.
FOR TGI FRIDAY, FOCUS ON CLASSIC EXPANSION WEEKS THAT OFTEN END WITH A FRIDAY PULLBACK.
WEEKLY PROFILES, WHEN COMBINED WITH PREVIOUS CONCEPTS SUCH AS ERL/IRL, CANDLE BODY CLOSES, SMT DIVERGENCE, AND INSTITUTIONAL ORDER FLOW (OF), HELP REMOVE MUCH OF THE SUBJECTIVITY IN TRADING AND PROVIDE A CLEAR, STRUCTURED FRAMEWORK.

CHART EXPLANATION (REFINED)
THIS EXAMPLE ILLUSTRATES HOW CLASSIC EXPANSION AND THURSDAY REVERSAL PROFILES TYPICALLY UNFOLD THROUGHOUT THE WEEK.
CLASSIC EXPANSION — LOW OF THE WEEK
THE LOW OF THE WEEK IS COMMONLY FORMED ON MONDAY OR TUESDAY.
EARLY-WEEK PRICE ACTION IS OFTEN ACCUMULATIVE OR CORRECTIVE, SETTING THE FOUNDATION FOR EXPANSION.
ONCE THE LOW IS ESTABLISHED, PRICE BEGINS TO EXPAND IN THE WEEKLY BIAS DIRECTION.

MIDWEEK BEHAVIOR
AFTER THE LOW IS SET, PRICE CONTINUES HIGHER WITH CONTROLLED PULLBACKS.
THESE PULLBACKS USUALLY RESPECT PDAS / HTF ARRAYS, CONFIRMING CONTINUATION RATHER THAN REVERSAL.

THURSDAY REVERSAL
IN SOME WEEKS, PRICE REACHES A TEMPORARY EXTREME AND SHOWS A THURSDAY REVERSAL.
THIS OFTEN HAPPENS AFTER LIQUIDITY IS TAKEN AT A SHORT-TERM HIGH OR LOW.
THURSDAY ACTS AS A DECISION DAY: EITHER CONTINUATION RESUMES OR DISTRIBUTION BEGINS.

CLASSIC EXPANSION — HIGH OF THE WEEK
THE HIGH OF THE WEEK FREQUENTLY FORMS ON MONDAY OR TUESDAY IN BEARISH EXPANSIONS, OR LATER IN THE WEEK AFTER STRONG BULLISH CONTINUATION.
ONCE THE WEEKLY OBJECTIVE IS REACHED, MOMENTUM SLOWS, SIGNALING POTENTIAL DISTRIBUTION OR RETRACEMENT.

FRIDAY BEHAVIOR
IN TGI FRIDAY SCENARIOS, PRICE OFTEN RETRACES BACK INTO THE WEEKLY RANGE AFTER THE TARGET IS MET.
THIS PULLBACK IS NOT GUARANTEED EVERY WEEK — CONTEXT AND HTF NARRATIVE REMAIN CRITICAL.
FRIDAY SHOULD BE TRADED CAUTIOUSLY, FOCUSING ON PROFIT PROTECTION RATHER THAN AGGRESSIVE ENTRIES.

KEY TAKEAWAY
WEEKLY PROFILES HELP TRADERS:
ANTICIPATE WHEN HIGHS AND LOWS ARE LIKELY TO FORM
ALIGN ENTRIES WITH INSTITUTIONAL TIMING
AVOID OVERTRADING AGAINST THE WEEKLY NARRATIVE
WHEN USED CORRECTLY, THEY PROVIDE STRUCTURE, TIMING, AND CLARITY TO THE TRADING WEEK.

## 📄 หน้า 3: DAILY BIAS CHEAT SHEET

### 1. ส่วนของข้อความ
DAILY BIAS CHEAT SHEET
The main objective of defining a daily bias is to determine the likelihood of the next candle being bullish or bearish.
The key question is: Why is today or tomorrow a high-probability day for price to deliver to a target aligned with our bias?
I use the following five core concepts to define my daily bias.

DAILY BIAS
---
Framing a Narrative
Where is price coming from?
Where is price likely to go next?

Does the market have a reason to:
Move higher to target buyside liquidity, or lower to target sellside liquidity?
Move up or down to rebalance an imbalance?

1. Internal & External Range Liquidity
Are we moving from Internal Range Liquidity to External Range Liquidity?
Or from External Range Liquidity back to Internal Range Liquidity?
This helps determine whether price is in continuation mode or mean reversion.

2. Candle Body Close (PDH/L & PWH/L)
Did price close beyond the previous day’s high or low?
Did price sweep the previous high/low and close back inside the range?
Do we have an Inside Day candle?
Candle closes provide critical insight into directional strength or weakness.

3. Institutional Order Flow
Do we currently have a bullish or bearish market structure?
Is price respecting or violating PD Arrays in alignment with the current direction?
This confirms whether price is aligned with institutional flow.

4. SMT Divergence
Do we have an SMT Divergence with a highly correlated market?
SMT helps identify potential weakness, strength, or upcoming reversals.

5. Weekly Profiles
Have we confirmed the High or Low of the Week?
Weekly profiles align the daily bias with the higher-timeframe narrative.

Conclusion
Do we have a clear Draw on Liquidity, bullish or bearish?
If not, remaining neutral is the correct decision.

Daily Profile #2: New York Reversal
This explanation focuses only on the London Session and New York AM Session.

Core Concept
NY Reversal is a daily profile where:
London fails to reach a key HTF level
The true Low or High of the Day is formed during New York
New York becomes the main reversal and expansion session
This profile commonly appears when London creates compression or inefficiency, not a full expansion.

Bullish Scenario Overview
In a bullish bias, the sequence generally looks like this:
Price opens at midnight
London trades lower but fails to reach a HTF Discount Array
The real Low of the Day (LOD) is expected after NY opens
New York then creates a reversal and expansion higher for the rest of the session

London Session (Approx. 2:00 - 5:00 AM)
What we want to see during London:
Price trades below the midnight open
Market hovers above the HTF Discount Array
No strong displacement or confirmation
Indicates London is not the main driver
👉 In short:
London = setup phase, not reversal

New York AM Session
Expected behavior:
After the NY Open / CME Open (8:30 or 9:30 AM)
Price pushes lower into a 4H PD Array
Enters a nested ITF + M15/H1 PD Array
Strong reaction confirms:
Low of the Day
M15 CISD / MSS
After confirmation:
Price expands higher
Targets a Higher Timeframe Premium Array
Expansion often continues for the remainder of NY AM

High / Low of the Day in New York
If the Low of the Day is confirmed during NY (bullish case):
The High of the Day typically forms:
Between 2:00 - 3:00 PM
Sometimes extends into 4:00 PM

Core Concept
NY Manipulation & Expansion is a daily profile where:
The London Session consolidates or accumulates
The main manipulation occurs during New York
After manipulation, price delivers a strong expansion in line with the bias

This profile is a clear example of AMD / Power of 3: Accumulation -> Manipulation -> Distribution (Expansion)

When the market has a bullish bias, the typical sequence is:
Price opens from the midnight open
During London, price remains in consolidation
London does not trade into a key HTF Discount Array
During New York, price is manipulated lower
This manipulation prints the Low of the Day
After confirmation, price expands higher for the rest of the session

London Session
Key characteristics of London:
Price trades sideways (accumulation)
No strong displacement into discount zones
No meaningful structure confirmation
Confirms that London is not the reversal or expansion session
👉 In short:
London = accumulation phase

After the NY Open / CME Open (8:30 or 9:30 AM)

Daily Profile #4: New York Judas

Core Concept
New York Judas is a daily profile where:
The market gives a false directional move during London
Price appears to follow the higher-timeframe bias
During New York, price makes a sharp move in the opposite direction (the Judas swing)
After the trap, price reverses back into the true bias and expands strongly

This profile is often called the "Judas Swing", as it traps early participants before the real move begins.

Bullish Scenario Overview
When the market has a bullish bias, the typical sequence is:
Price opens from the midnight open
During London, price moves higher in line with the bias
Price does not trade into a HTF Discount Array
The London move builds engineered liquidity above price
During New York:
Price drops sharply
Sweeps intraday liquidity
Trades into a 4H / HTF Discount PD Array
A strong bullish reversal follows

London Session

Price rises without a healthy pullback
No clear manipulation into discount
The move is designed to:
Attract late buyers
Create liquidity above price

London = fake continuation / liquidity engineering
